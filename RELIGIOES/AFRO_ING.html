Afro-Brazilian Religions

About this religion:

According to the latest IBGE census, 1.6% of the Brazilian population follows religions Umbanda and Candomblé (The IGBE did not separate religions, it only gave a general term to represent all ). These religions arose in the slavery period, when millions of Africans arrived and there was a mixture cultural, religious and linguistic. It was the religion that suffered most (and still suffers) religious intolerance. Umbanda it is a mixture of the cult of the Orixás with elements of indigenous religions and a certain syncretism with Catholicism. Already Candomblé is a religion that remains very faithful to the origins of African religions.
